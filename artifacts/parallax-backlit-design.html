Backlit Design LanguageLight = Data

The glow isn't decoration — it's information

Every glowing element in Parallax carries temperature data. A red backlight means "heated" before you read a single word. A teal glow means "this is the calm translation." The intensity and color of ambient light around borders, badges, pills, and the signal rail all encode emotional temperature. Your eye is drawn to the brightest points — and those points are always the most emotionally significant.

The Three Glow Layers
Layer 1: Ambient

Subtle radial gradients on the background. Temperature pools of color that warm or cool the canvas.

Layer 2: Backlit
Glow radiates outward

Temperature-colored light bleeding away from content into the margin. The border is the light source — glow radiates outward, words stay clean.

Layer 3: Halo
COOLING DOWN

Text glow and box-shadow halos on badges, pills, and dots. Light radiates from focal points.

Design Rule

Glow intensity follows temperature. Hotter messages get stronger, wider glow. Cool messages get softer, tighter glow. Neutral messages have no glow at all — just flat borders. This way the visual "noise" of the page mirrors the emotional intensity of the conversation. A calm conversation looks dim and peaceful. A heated exchange literally illuminates the page.
