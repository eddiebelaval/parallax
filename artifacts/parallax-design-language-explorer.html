Parallax Design Language Explorer

Four palette directions for a real-time conflict resolution tool. Each is designed around the core constraint: supporting an emotional temperature system (hot to cool) while feeling safe enough for vulnerable conversations and insightful enough for NVC analysis.

The Four Directions

Each palette is tuned for the same product constraints: dark-mode-first, emotional temperature visualization, Person A/B distinction, and NVC analysis reveal. The question is feeling — what emotion should the container provoke before anyone says a word?

Deep Water

Navy/indigo foundation. Trust, depth, calm. The ocean floor where things settle.

Warm Instrument

Refined Factory. Rich warm charcoal, terracotta accents, lavender for insight. The precision tool with a heartbeat.

Dusk

Deep plum/aubergine. The softening hour where vulnerability becomes beautiful. Gold illuminates what's hidden.

Ember

Deep brown warmth. Sitting by a fire, having the hard conversation. Amber honey light reveals unmet needs.

Deep Water

Trust + Depth

"The ocean floor where things settle"

Navy/indigo creates immediate psychological trust (the most research-backed "empathy color"). Coral warmth for emotional heat feels natural — like warm currents in deep water. The palette draws from the Mediterranean coast palette: terra cotta, sand, sage, deep sea.

Vulnerability + Beauty

"The golden hour — when defenses soften"
