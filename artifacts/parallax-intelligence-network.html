The Intelligence Network changes this. Each user builds a persistent profile through a conversational interview. The Enrichment Interview

A conversational AI interview that builds a comprehensive behavioral profile in 10-15 minutes. Audio-first, clinically-informed, never clinical-feeling.

Key Finding
AI conversational interviews outperform paper questionnaires in agreement with clinical assessment — kappa 0.72 vs 0.55. People disclose more to AI than to forms.
Design Principle
This is NOT a diagnostic assessment. It's a conversation that happens to capture clinically-useful patterns. The user should feel heard, not evaluated.
Phase 1 Context Setting
3-4 min / 4 questions
Establishes safety, explains what data is captured and how it's protected. Sets the tone: conversational, not clinical. Captures basic conflict context — who are the key relationships in your life, what brings you here.

Phase 2 Communication Profiling
3-4 min / 5 questions
Explores communication style under stress. Uses validated instruments (ECR-RS for attachment, TKI-inspired for conflict modes) but delivered conversationally — never "on a scale of 1-5."

Phase 3 Context-Specific Deep Dive
3-4 min / 4 questions
Adapts based on the user's primary conflict context. Intimate relationships get Gottman Four Horsemen questions. Professional contexts get SCARF and Organizational Justice probes. Family gets intergenerational pattern exploration.

The most human phase. "Tell me about a conflict that sticks with you." Free-form narrative capture that reveals patterns the structured questions miss. The AI extracts implicit values, unstated needs, and recurring themes.

The Anonymization Boundary

A one-way function separates personal narratives from behavioral signals. You can derive signals from data — you can never reconstruct data from signals.

Private Zone AES-256-GCM / RLS / User-Only
Narrative "My ex-wife used to give me the silent treatment for days. Now when my partner goes quiet, I panic."
Trigger Detail "Being left on read triggers abandonment anxiety from childhood."
Relationship Data "Partner: Alex, 3 years, live together, co-parenting his daughter."
Self-Report "I know I get clingy when I'm scared they're pulling away."
History "Divorced in 2022. Father was emotionally unavailable."
ONE-WAY FUNCTION
Signal Zone Anonymized / Consent-Gated
Attachment Anxious-preoccupied. High activation on perceived withdrawal.
Conflict Mode Accommodating primary, Avoiding secondary. Low competing threshold.
Gottman Risk Pursuer pattern. Escalation risk when partner stonewalls.
Regulation Emotional flooding onset: 60-90 seconds under rejection cues.
Values Security, connection, verbal reassurance. SCARF: high Relatedness.
The One-Way Guarantee
Behavioral signals are extracted through Claude Structured Outputs — a classification and scoring process, not a summarization. The output is categorical (e.g., "anxious-preoccupied", not "has abandonment issues from childhood"). You can know someone's attachment style without knowing WHY they have it.

The test: Given only the signals on the right side, can you reconstruct the narratives on the left? No. You know what patterns exist — never why they exist, who they involve, or what happened. This is the privacy guarantee.

Security Architecture

Trust is the product. Every layer is designed so that a breach of any single component cannot expose personal narratives.

AES-256-GCM Encryption at Rest
All raw profile data encrypted before storage. Keys derived per-user. Even a database dump yields only ciphertext.
Row-Level Security (RLS) Isolation
Supabase RLS policies ensure users can only read their own profiles. No admin backdoor. No bulk export API.
Consent-Gated Signal Sharing
Anonymous signals only flow to the other party's Claude context when BOTH parties explicitly opt in. Per-session consent. Revocable anytime.
Audit Trail
Every signal access is logged: who accessed, when, what session, what consent level was active. Users can review their audit log anytime.
Safety Rails
Abuse detection, "Are you safe?" private check, crisis resources (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, NDVH), asymmetric exit (either party can leave without the other knowing), 18+ age gate.

What We Did Right

Research
Comprehensive research foundation. The User Intelligence Layer research doc covers psychology (validated instruments), competitive analysis (the empty quadrant), privacy model (one-way mirror), safety rails (cautionary tales), and technical architecture. Building from evidence, not assumptions.
Architecture
Privacy-by-design, not privacy-as-afterthought. The anonymization boundary is architectural, not policy-based. Signals are classifications, not summaries. You can't "prompt inject" your way to someone's raw narrative because the raw narrative never enters the signal extraction pipeline.
Positioning
"Conflict resolution" not "therapy." Avoids HIPAA/FDA/CMIA regulatory burden entirely. Lessons from Replika, Woebot, and BetterHelp baked into the design from day one.
