Light Mode Philosophy

Not a simple invert. The light mode is warm parchment — #f5efe6 has a yellow-brown undertone, not blue-white. Think: aged paper, natural linen, a counselor's notepad. The accents deepen by ~15% to maintain WCAG 4.5:1 contrast on the warm background.

ASCII Art + MotionBotanical

"Organic growth, not mechanical precision"

Botanical ASCII art as texture — vines, leaves, blooming forms. The motifs are metaphoric: growth, unfurling, branching out. They breathe with subtle animation. Used as background texture (very low opacity), loading states, section dividers, and the "waiting for partner" state.

Implementation Notes

The parallax effect uses CSS sticky positioning and transforms. The ASCII botanical texture stays fixed at a slower scroll rate while content slides over it. On mobile, the parallax degrades gracefully to a static background — performance over spectacle.

Full Design Language SummaryEmber + Botanical + Parallax

"Firelight, growth, and shifting perspectives"

The Ember system in three sentences: Deep chocolate warmth creates safety for vulnerable conversations. Botanical ASCII art — breathing, growing, unfurling — embodies the organic nature of conflict resolution (not mechanical, not clinical). Parallax scrolling reinforces the product's core idea: the same conversation looks different depending on your depth of view.
Dark Mode

Deep chocolate base. Amber illumination. The default — for intimate, focused sessions. "Firelight conversation."

Light Mode

Warm parchment base. Deep brown text. For daytime use, accessibility. "Morning light through linen."

Shared System

Temperature scale, person colors, NVC green, ASCII motifs — identical semantics in both modes.

The Three Pillars
1. Ember Palette

What: Warm chocolate dark + parchment light, amber/coral/eucalyptus accents.

Why: Safety. People need to feel held before they can be honest.

2. Botanical ASCII

What: Organic vine/fern/bloom motifs rendered in ASCII, breathing with subtle animation.

Why: Growth metaphor. Conflict resolution is organic, not mechanical.

3. Parallax Scroll

What: Multi-layer depth on landing page — ASCII texture behind, content middle, accent foreground.

Why: Namesake. Same conversation, different depths — the product's core insight.
